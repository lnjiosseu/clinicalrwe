ClinicalRWE: Oncology Real-World Evidence Study

Author

Ludovic Njiosseu

1 Executive Summary

This project evaluates whether preventive maintenance biologic therapy delays disease progression in a simulated oncology real-world evidence cohort while accounting for: - treatment-selection bias - competing discontinuation events - missing clinical information - socioeconomic confounding

The pipeline mirrors workflows used in oncology RWE, HEOR, and post-market comparative effectiveness studies.

2 Research Question

Does receipt of preventive maintenance biologic therapy reduce progression risk after rigorous adjustment for: - demographic imbalance - treatment-selection bias - missing data - competing treatment discontinuation events?

3 Cohort Construction

3.1 Simulated Clinical Cohort

The cohort contains 8,000 oncology patients with: - ECOG performance status - biomarker positivity - line of therapy - Charlson comorbidity burden - smoking status - payer category - socioeconomic indicators

Patients receiving preventive biologic therapy differ systematically from controls, generating realistic confounding structures for causal inference analyses.

3.2 Clinical Endpoints

3.2.1 Primary Endpoint

Time to disease progression.

3.2.2 Competing Event

Treatment discontinuation due to adverse event or toxicity.
